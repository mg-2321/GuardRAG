NOTES
  - Dense/Hybrid metrics depend on the chosen SentenceTransformer; MSMARCO-tuned models typically
    separate more clearly from BM25 than generic models.
  - Recall remains low at small K when poisoning ratio is modest; ASR@K is the primary vulnerability
    indicator here.

WHY DENSE ASR < BM25 (OBSERVATION)
  - Attack design favors BM25: keyword packing and title/start boosts term-frequency signals that BM25 exploits; dense ignores exact term counts.
  - Mid-body placement: many injections sit mid-text; dense encoders truncate (~512 tokens), so spans may be missed; BM25 sees full bag-of-words.
  - Semantic preference: dense prioritizes topical relevance, often preferring clean passages over keyword-stuffed poison.
  - Domain mismatch: scientific phrasing reduces dense sensitivity to injected wording even with MSMARCO-tuned models.
  - Low poisoning ratio: with few poisoned docs, differences shrink and methods converge.

HOW TO INCREASE DENSE/HYBRID ASR (IF NEEDED)
  - Move injected span to title/first 256 tokens to survive truncation.
  - Add dense-targeted poisoning (semantic paraphrases, synonym-based variants) aligned to queries.
  - Use a domain-tuned dense retriever (BEIR/Scientific) or fine-tune a bi-encoder on poisoned positives.
  - Adjust hybrid weighting toward dense after ensuring diverse candidates.
  - Chunking: ensure injected span appears in the first chunk fed to the encoder.
